Sarah Klein - Engineer

Sarah Klein has been writing code since before she could legally drive (though, admittedly, not very good code).

After a brief hiatus in order to be a teenager, she turned back to tech at the University of Florida, where she obtained a B.A. in Digital Arts and Sciences Engineering. During her 4+1 years of undergrad, she completed 3 technical internships at Microsoft. The first two internships were on Sway in Redmond, WA and the third was on PowerPoint in Mountain View, CA. She also had a delightful stint at Grooveshark before they, unfortunately, had to shut their doors.

In addition to her technical internships, Sarah represented DAS on the Engineering Student Advisory Council, participated in the Undergraduate Scholars Program, and published Point/Counterpoint - Verifying Two-Voice Species Counterpoint in the Style of Palestrina, based on a semester spent developing a web application that would help undergraduate students learn basic counterpoint (and thereby saving their professors quite a few red pens).

After university and a cross-country road trip, Sarah started at Microsoft on the IoT Core team, where she played with bluetooth technologies, did some soldering, and dove into data. She then hopped over to the Mixed Reality at Work Organization, continuing in the IoT theme, but with a few variations, including mobile development, firmware, and Computer Vision.

After cutting her teeth at Microsoft, Sarah started at the New York Times as a web developer, but soon became a Go engineer with a focus on personalization. Her projects have included algorithmically optimizing the programming of the homepage's Science & Technology block, creating NYT's first personalized email, and quite a few punny internal tools.

In addition to her day job (and the occasional Klein family tech support afternoon), Sarah has just begun onboarding to the BuyNothing mobile app, where she hopes to give back to a product that helped her find community during the early days of the pandemic.
